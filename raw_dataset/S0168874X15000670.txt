@&#ABSTRACT@&#


                  A microgripper component which is made of piezo-cantilever is presented in this paper. The modified mixed variational principle including piezoelectric materials is established and then the state-vector equations of laminated piezoelectric beams are derived directly from the principle. Finally the exact expressions of mechanical and electrical variables along the thickness direction based on the state-vector equation are simply given. Based on the semi-analytical solution of the state-vector equation, a state space finite element model is established. The static analysis is carried out for a laminated beam fully covered with piezoelectric actuating layers on the top and bottom surfaces of the substrate. Both the substrate and piezoelectricity of the laminated beam are considered as one-dimensional line solids and the same linear element is used to discretize the whole beam. This semi-analytical technique can guarantee the continuity of generalized displacements and generalized stresses inter-layers and the transfer matrix method can be applied. Furthermore the exact solution in one direction ensures there is no restriction on the thickness for the smart structures. At the end of this paper the influence of secondary converse piezoelectric effect (SCPE) on the deflection of the laminated beam covered with piezoelectric driving layers for static precision displacement control is investigated. The above mentioned state space finite element method is used to predict the deflection of the beam with and without SCPE. If the influence of SCPE is eliminated, the deflections of the clamped beam will increase by ~2.7%. In addition, the driving characteristic with different PZT/steel thickness ratio considering SCPE are also studied. The maximum influence of SCPE on deflection can reach ~3% for the beam fully covered with piezoelectric active layers, which should not be neglected in practical microoperation applications.
               

@&#INTRODUCTION@&#

Due to their successful and wide application in microoperation systems [1], dynamic and static control of structure shape [2], vibration noise [3] and health monitoring of structures [4], the laminated piezoelectric smart structures or the conventional structures with piezoelectric actuator and sensor patches have received a particular attention recently. Many studies on the electromechanical behaviors of piezoelectric structures have been carried out [5–8]. As a typical actuating element, the piezoelectric actuator is popularly used as one of the key components of the piezo-microgripper comprising of two parallel piezoelectric cantilevers [9] or the nano-positioning walking mechanism including piezoelectric beams with hinged ends [10]. When the electric voltage is applied on the piezoelectric layer, the resultant bending motion in the structure becomes the working principle in micro-mechanical applications.

An important characteristic of bending laminated piezoelectric structures is that the deflection of the bender is dependent on the driving voltage. One of the widely used methods for the laminated piezoelectric structures is the analytical solution. The exact solutions can predict precisely the static and dynamical behavior of the laminates, which are of great value. For the exact method the quantities near or across the interfaces of different material layers can be achieved, which would be employed as benchmark to determine the accuracy and effectiveness of various numerical methods for other more complex structures [11–13]. Among the exact solution strategies, the method based on the Eshelby–Stroh formulation or stator-vector equation [14–18] is widely used to investigate the responses of the general laminate structures or smart structures with piezoelectric under the external static or dynamical mechanical or electrical loads. The Eshelby–Stroh formulation method, also called mixed state variable means, regards the general displacements and stresses as the variable vector components at the same time. The transfer matrix in the solution of the state vector equation makes the final algebra equations independent of the laminate thickness and the layer number. Then based on the independent spatial variable such as the thickness parameter of the beams, plates and so on, the mixed state variable technology can be used to investigate the behavior of the laminate structures comprising different materials and complicated geometries [19,20]. Furthermore this provides a continuous stresses field across the thickness for the multi-layer structures which is impossible for many other numerical methods [14,21].

The complexity of the material, geometry and boundary conditions makes the governing equations in piezoelectric laminates too complicated to be solved analytically. Thus the numerical technologies, such as finite element method, boundary element method etc., are inevitably required to analyze the electromechanical coupling behaviors in piezoelectric structures. Most of the finite element methods for piezoelectric structures are developed from the traditional ones. It always makes the computational model artificially large and stiff when the piezoelectric smart devices and large structure regions are modeled using the three-dimensional brick elements. For the threadlike or thin piezoelectric structures, one- or two-dimensional elements [22] can be used with high efficiency and accuracy. Most of the elements analyzing the piezoelectric devices are free of electric degree of freedoms which are deduced based on the specific sensing/actuating equations [23]. So the operations for the inverse matrix are usually carried out and the numerical stability problems exist. In one word, many obstacles challenge the efficiency and accuracy in numerically exploring the responses of the piezoelectric structures to the environments.

The semi-analytical method can touch the balance between the accuracy, efficiency and the computational scale. The state space finite element method [24,25] conflates the conventional finite element technology and the state-vector equation means. The Hamilton element [24] adapts the finite element approximation in one dimension direction and introduces the analytical expression in the other direction. The state space finite element [26] assumes previously the analytical expressions satisfying the boundary conditions and solves the field functions in the finite element space. Hence the semi-analytical method reserves most of the advantages inhered in the exact solution based on the state-vector equations. Using the Hamiltonian element, the number of variables of the final algebra equation system is much less than the traditional finite element method. At the same time, the number of variables is independent of the thickness. So theoretically the semi-analytical technology can analyze the laminated structures with any number of layers. Additionally, the semi-analytical solution can ensure the continuousness of the transverse stresses between all the laminated interfaces [12]. It is worthwhile to find a semi-analytical method combining the analytic and finite element ascendency for discerning the couple phenomena in complicated piezoelectric multilayer structures.

Before the smart device fabrication, the piezoelectric actuator is always polarized in advance. And the SCPE [27] would exist in the actuator while applying mechanical or electrical loads, which could affect the actuator performance. Though the SCPE phenomena was researched theoretically and experimentally a long time ago [28], the influence of the SCPE is rarely considered in practical structures. Kapuria and Hagedorn [29] investigated the static and dynamic response of smart beams with segmented extension/shear mode bonded with piezoelectric actuators. And the induced voltage distribution along the beam thickness direction was calculated. The SCPE phenomenon produced by the induced voltages was neglected by the authors. In the study of piezoelectric sandwich beam, a quadratic variation of E
                     
                        z
                      across the thickness have been reported, of which the researchers explained as the attribution of the induced potential due to the direct piezoelectric effect [30]. But they did not do any detailed investigation about this situation either. This study focuses on the influence of the SCPE on the beam deformation, based on which, the elimination can be applied to improve the deflection.

This paper presents a state space finite element method that combines the advantages of the traditional finite element approximation techniques. The analytical solution means of the recursive formulation of state equation is applied to solve the mechanical problems of piezoelectric laminated beams. The method is based on a modified mixed variational principle that takes into account the variables of both general displacements and general stresses. To solve the variational equations, a laminated beam is discretized resembling the conventional finite elements method in the longitudinal direction of the beam, while the through-thickness distributions of generalized displacements and stresses are solved directly from the state equation analytically. Finally the semi-analytical method is used to predict the deflection of the beam with and without SCPE. The results show that the influence of the SCPE on the deflection of the beam is large and should not be neglected in practical applications.

@&#CONCLUSIONS@&#

A semi-analytical Hamilton element method has been presented to solve the mechanical problems of laminated beams fully covered with piezoelectric actuating layers. The method was based on a mixed variational representation including the electro-mechanical coupling equations. The generalized displacements and stresses in one direction were discretized by finite element technique while their cross thickness distributions were obtained by solving the state equations. The compatibility of generalized displacements and stresses across material interfaces are maintained by uniting the algebraic equation including both of them.

Numerical tests and comparisons have been carried out to validate the presented method. By comparing with existing piezoelectric bimorph analytical solutions, it was observed that the new method shown an excellent agreement. Since the transfer matrix technique was used to derive the state equations of laminated piezoelectric beam, the dimension of the final state equation had no restriction on the thickness or number of matrix layers or piezoelectric layer.

The influence of SCPE on the deflection of the beam fully covered with piezoelectric materials was also investigated. The semi-analytical mathematical model was established to predict the deflections of the laminated beam with and without SCPE. The results show that the deflections of the beam increase by about 3% while the influence of the SCPE being eliminated. The influence of SCPE on deflection increases as the thickness ratio h
                     
                        pzt
                     /h
                     
                        steel
                      (h
                     
                        pzt

                      is below 0.5) increases. In summary, the influence of the SCPE for intelligent structures fully covered with piezoelectric materials should not be neglected, and better to be eliminated to improve the actuator performance in practical actuating applications such as shape control, vibration suppression and so on.
